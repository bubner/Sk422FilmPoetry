Series of Poems | 'Shrouded by Torment'

Written by Lucas Bubner

Torment | Victim

Unlying creation through the portal of negation,
we find the missing and needed debation.
Suffering in which this ridden pearl,
a mental war in the cyberworld.
And as I stow and leap as a freeman,
I’m come to fear with the demon.


I’m lost in despair and solemn breath,
how can one hide from death?
Why should I rise with given sight
if yet I’m going to suffer endless fright?


I’ve lost all sense and will to survive,
numb to the stories those contrive.
I must hold on for hope to arrive,
at the scope of writing the deep archive.


Torment | Victim's Parent

Looking down, I’m lost in the net,
of serious debate and questions as yet.
The world looks upon me as I’ve done no harm,
but still finds me as guilty as a firearm.


Day after day, I return in dismay,
finding my child has been a puppet at play.
It is due now and profound for that child to depart,
but I can’t set the values that those have taken heart.


It is not my doing in this insufferable fate;
it was only caused by the comrades we hate.
But under the surface and into the lore,
why does my child have to fall into great war?


The help and support of the ones at the shore,
may continue to help stop such uproar,
But whether they do or fail to abide,
the scars will remain under hidden confide.


Torment | Principal

Observing the gates of chaos and the mind,
I sense the oncoming of those sand-blind.
The eternal ransom of impending doom,
will come forth and apply everlasting gloom.


I look at those that have now come to ferment;
Ah, the memories, a war of torment.
The mind and body may call it a gift at the finish,
but the race is far from over for the soul who had diminished.


The soul and spirit are a construct at one,
simple words that cannot be outrun;
Moving forth, my witness is in despair,
but alas, time simply cannot repair.


As the tides spin, my role succeeds;
the court of life still proceeds.
And as one finds their humility in mind,
they will seek their undefined.


Torment | Bully

The stars continue to shine, our stellar missing pledge;
reality can’t comprehend, the knife cutting edge.
A free reign of words, hidden in disguise,
a true hidden meaning, a world despised.


The light from the stars doesn’t give a return,
even from the worst sunburn.
I can’t have back what I lost in the storm,
losing myself stuck as a hopeless life form.


I can’t find a way out from this endless loop,
I grieve and miss but continue to scoop.
The damages I’ve done to the people in my oath,
have lost their light in their mental growth.


My sky is darkened, and it’s impossible to guide,
but no one’s here to stop the flood tide.
I wish I could stop, but the voice continues to roll,
“No one can save you from this loophole.”


Torment | External Observer

I watch on the horizon as the ground begins to shake,
it appears another soul is at the hostile stake.
I cannot interfere in the rights of the wave,
I can merely look and gaze upon the brave.


Forever days will pass as the plague ensues,
one man’s actions cannot diffuse.
The humble in this world will condemn the many,
but are still unable to defeat the plenty.


One must only hope the abuse will end,
but will never be able to curve the bend.
Our plane of existence is coated in remorse;
Don’t get lost, you’ve hardly finished the course.
