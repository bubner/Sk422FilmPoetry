Series of Poems | 'Shrouded by Torment'

Written by Lucas Bubner

×

"Unlying creation through the portal of negation,"
The 'portal of negation' refers to the general life found at school or places hated other than home, a portal to somewhere filled with 'creations of negativity'.

"missing and needed debation."
This implies that in our contemporary society, there's a lot of missing rules and guidelines causing prejudice.

"ridden pearl,"
Refers to our planet Earth.

"mental war in the cyberworld."
Relates to cyberbullying as 'a war in the cyberworld'

"stow and leap as a freeman,"
'stow' refers to logging off from the internet, where the cyberbullying is; and 'leaping as a freeman' refers to attempting to assert 'freedom' from the cyberwar.

"death?"
Exaggeration of the bully's effects on the victim, can be linked to suicide.

"suffer endless fright?"
The victim cannot go a single day without dealing with bullies.

"stories those contrive."
Rumours, and false propaganda about the victim.

"writing the deep archive."
Refers to the government permanent records for abusive behaviours

"serious debate and questions as yet."
A lot of questions are unanswered for the parent, they don't know the full story.

"guilty as a firearm."
Although the parent hasn't done anything, they're still as lawfully guilty for their child's health similar to owning illegal weaponry.

"return in dismay,"
These set of events continue through each day, with seemingly no end.

"can’t set the values"
Cannot control the bully's morals or motives

"caused by the comrades we hate."
Refers to the predicted reasoning why their child is continually getting bullied. It has a similar effect for the parent alike the child.

"why does my child have to fall into great war?"
Feeling responsible that their child is getting bullied, and is getting upset from the inside about it.

"ones at the shore,"
Those who are indirectly involved in the war at sea.

"scars will remain under hidden confide."
Even if the bullying stops, the scars will remain on their mental health; a secret that must be kept in close.

"gates of chaos and the mind,"
Refers to the schoolground or workplace environment which is the 'playing field for the mind'.

"sand-blind."
Refers to those who were caught under the effects of bullying

"ransom of impending doom,"
Infers to blackmails, continual harassment, and other forms of abuse.

"everlasting gloom."
Indicates that it is a very tough and saddening matter to deal with in any circumstance.

"have now come to ferment;"
Those who are growing up or maturing.

"memories,"
A silent inference that can potentially indicate that the principal may also have gone down this same fate previously.

"gift at the finish,"
After the bullying is over, it might feel like a positive lesson or relief

"soul who had diminished."
Although the bullying is over, the scars on the soul do not heal.

"simple words that cannot be outrun;"
Every small word that goes to the soul builds up over time and bottles up.

"my witness is in despair,"
Refers to the investigation between the bully and victim, knowing exactly how the victim is feeling.

"but alas, time simply cannot repair."
No matter what the principal does, they cannot heal the scars done on the soul.

"my role succeeds;"
Regardless of whatever they do, they will still continue to uphold the role of the principal.

"the court of life still proceeds."
Refers to the battles and wars we all have in life, where the principal can only do so much against it.

"seek their undefined."
Refers to the bully 'finding their deeper meaning,' or realising what they had been doing wrong and start to feel the consequences of their actions.

"stellar missing pledge;"
Infers to the missing and unknown questions of our universe.

"reality can’t comprehend, the knife cutting edge."
What we define as reality is often painful, sharp, and full of things that hurt.

"a world despised."
Dictates that there is a deeper backstory behind the bully; they aren't bullying just because they can.

"even from the worst sunburn."
There are no escapes from the pain and suffering in this world. There will always be times where life is going to hurt.

"lost in the storm,"
Lost in their guidance, doesn't know how to keep moving forward.

"continue to scoop."
They continue to bully (and 'scoop' at the victim) even when they don't want to anymore.

"my oath,"
The people who were affected by the bully's actions.

"stop the flood tide."
No one wants to help the bully; this can be for a multitude of reasons, having to do with rudeness or pushing away those who try to help.

"No one can save you from this loophole."
The voice in their head has lost all hope knowing that there is no escape from this infinite cycle.

"appears another soul is at the hostile stake."
They witness yet another person under the effects of bullying.

"interfere in the rights of the wave,"
They can't intervene with the situation, even if they wanted to.

"gaze upon said ‘brave’."
The observer can only view upon the situation, and watch those who are brave and decide to go in.

"plague ensues,"
Bullyig is similar to a contagious plague; there is a never-ending replication of it which is extremely difficult to contain.

"one man’s actions cannot diffuse."
The influence of one man (the observer) cannot stop all the effects of bullying in our society.

"humble in this world will condemn the many,"
Those who try to fight against bullying will try to stop it, but will never be able to.

"defeat the plenty."
There are too many emerging bullies to eliminate the problem of bullying in our society. It is simply an aspect that we must deal with in our contemporary society.

"curve the bend."
The amount of changes required to eliminate bullying and harassment in our world is too high, and now has become something we must live with.

"coated in remorse;"
Regret, pain, and the struggles of life coat the entire plane of existence. We cannot live in our society without these things.

"hardly finished the course."
There will always be something around the next corner waiting to hurt you. This is the way of our contemporary society as a whole, and we must learn to deal with that in our adolescence.
